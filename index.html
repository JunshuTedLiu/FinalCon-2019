The University of Chicago Final Week Conference

March 11 ~ 15, 2019

An exciting final week.

Starting March 11, 2019, students in the University of Chicago will say hello to the final weeks of the Winter 2019 quarter.

Junshu Liu, a master student in the Digital Studies program, will show us three exciting research progresses. He will introduce the updated version of the data analysis and visualization tool -KnoGlo 2.0; make a speech about IoT and its impacts, and present an NLP research project regarding the development of the Internet in the past 50 years.

SCHEDULE

March 11

Wieboldt Hall 111
10:30am - 11:20am

Knowledge Globe (KnoGlo) 2.0 Keynote

KnoGlo is a tool that allows users to analyze and visualize the distribution of research interests for the selected topic and year.

This quarter, we introduce the new KnoGlo 2.0 with the same feature but runs directly on the webpage that will deliver an intuitive and convenient user experience.


March 14

Oriental Institute 226C
3:00pm - 4:30pm

Speaker Series: IoT and how it will change everything

IoT is one of the most popular topics in the technology industry.

What is IoT? Except for the consumer market, who else is playing the game? How will it change people's lifestyle and even industries other than technology? And, for developers and designers: how it related to the study of HCI?


March 15

Social Science Rsch Bldg 401
9:30am - 10:20am

A dream of Silicon Valley: from '69 to '19

Using Natural Language Processing research technique, Let's take an in-depth look for the history of how computer and technology changes, especially the evolution of the Internet and its growing business, as well as how it changes every single industry other than tech industry and affects people's lifestyle.
